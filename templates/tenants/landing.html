Frequently asked questions

Is DERP really free and open source?

Yes. DERP is released under the MIT License — one of the most permissive open source licenses. You can use it for any purpose, including commercial use, with no fees and no contributor agreement to sign.

Can I self-host DERP on my own server?

Absolutely. The full source is on GitHub. You'll need Python 3.13+ and PostgreSQL 15+. The README has step-by-step instructions for getting it running locally or on a VPS like DigitalOcean, Hetzner, or Railway.

How does DERP compare to Odoo, ERPNext, or Dolibarr?

DERP is intentionally lightweight. It focuses on the core ERP loop — accounting, inventory, sales, purchasing, manufacturing — without the enterprise plugin sprawl. It's a single Django app with a clean codebase that a single developer can read in an afternoon.

What kind of business is DERP for?

Small and medium businesses that buy, sell, and possibly manufacture physical goods. Distributors, light manufacturers, retailers, ecommerce shops, and consultants who need real accounting plus inventory in one place.

Is my data safe on the hosted version?

Each workspace runs in its own isolated PostgreSQL schema — there is no shared table where tenant data lives together. HTTPS is enforced, passwords are hashed with Argon2, and you can export everything to CSV or JSON at any time.
